üèõÔ∏è The Davia Bargellini Museum

Located in a magnificent 17th-century Baroque palace, the Civic Museum of Industrial Arts and Davia Bargellini Picture Gallery represents one of Bologna's cultural gems. Opened to the public in 1924 under the guidance of art historian Francesco Malaguzzi Valeri, the museum preserves the authentic atmosphere of an 18th-century Bolognese apartment.

üìö The Collections

üñºÔ∏è Picture Gallery

Paintings from the Gothic to Baroque periods, with particular emphasis on Bolognese Renaissance artists such as Simone dei Crocifissi, Vitale da Bologna, and Michele de Matteo.

üè∫ Applied Arts

Elaborate ceramics, wrought iron works, historical keys, liturgical vestments, antique furniture, street theater marionettes, and a magnificent gilded carriage.

The museum, consisting of seven exhibition rooms, still maintains the original arrangement conceived by Malaguzzi Valeri, creating a unique atmosphere that Cilekler respects and enhances through technology.

üìã PACT Analysis Framework

The development of √áilekler was guided by a comprehensive PACT (People, Activities, Context, Technologies) analysis to ensure optimal user experience design and successful implementation.

üë• People

Primary Audience: Tourists visiting Bologna in small groups (2-4 people), characterized as "simply curious" visitors attracted by the museum's free entrance.

  • Mixed age groups with varying art/history knowledge
  • International visitors with diverse cultural backgrounds
  • Technology-comfortable smartphone users
  • Limited time for museum visits (30-60 minutes)
  • Seeking authentic yet accessible cultural experiences
  • Prefer collaborative discovery and discussion

üéØ Activities

Core Challenge: Transform passive museum observation into active, engaging learning experiences for casual visitors.

  • QR code scanning to access digital reproductions
  • "Spot the difference" gameplay with AI-modified artwork
  • Educational feedback through family avatars
  • Progressive difficulty across different artifacts
  • Point-based reward system with group celebration
  • Self-paced exploration allowing flexible timing
  • Multi-sensory interaction with audio and visual elements

üèõÔ∏è Context

Environment: Historic 17th-century Baroque palazzo with original interiors and period atmosphere.

  • Seven intimate exhibition rooms with period furniture
  • Variable lighting conditions (natural and artificial)
  • Stone and wood architecture with acoustic considerations
  • WiFi infrastructure throughout the museum
  • Multi-lingual international environment
  • Small group dynamics requiring quiet interaction
  • Part of Bologna's civic museum network
  • Free entrance policy attracting diverse visitors

üíª Technologies

Technical Implementation: Progressive Web App with cross-platform compatibility and offline capabilities.

  • PWA for iOS/Android compatibility
  • QR code scanning and verification system
  • AI-powered artwork modification algorithms
  • Interactive 3D avatar system with voice synthesis
  • Multi-language content management
  • Low-latency response for interactive elements
  • Optimized bandwidth usage for museum WiFi
  • Cross-browser compatibility and accessibility
